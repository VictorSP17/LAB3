Variance of each dimension¶

$X = [x_1, ..., x_N]$, is our dataset. Each $x_i$ is a movie of dimension $M$, with all the scores for the different $M$ tags.

We want to know the covariance matrix of a given movie $x$. We know that: $$\mathrm{Var}(x) = \mathbb{E}[(x-\mathbb{E}[x])^2]$$ Then, we will center our matrix (all the data points, i.e. columns) and then estimate the covariance matrix:

  1. $X = X - \overline{X}$
  2. $\mathrm{Var}(x) \approx \frac{1}{N}X X^T = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i = 1}^N x_i x_i^T$
In [9]:
X_center = X - X.mean(1).reshape([1128,1])
CX = (1/N)*np.matmul(X_center,np.transpose(X_center))

Implications for dimensionality reduction¶

The implications are that the variance is mostly accumulated in the directions of the first (highest) eigenvalues, which will allow us to draw a low dimensional space in which most of the variance will still be present. Basically we have that: $$X X^T = V D V^T$$ $$X X^T = \sum_{i = 1}^{M}\lambda_i v_i v_i^T$$ Where $V = [v_1,...,v_M]$ (ordered higher to smaller) are the eigenvectors and $D$ the eigenvalues ($\lambda_i$). Keeping only the highest eigenvalues with their eigenvectors will be able to reconstruct most of the covariance matrix. Furthermore, in that space the covariance matrix is $D$ and the eigenvalues are the directions capturing the variance. Since $D$ is diagonal there is no correlation between dimensions and all the variation is captured in the diagonal when we are in the $V$ space.

NOTE: All our eigenvalues are positive because the matrix is symetric (positive semidefinite).

Now let us see how many directions do we need in order to capture 2/3 of the total variance.

Project the movies on the first 2 components¶

How do we project the images in the first 2 principal components? Given that: $$V^T x_e = x_v$$ Meaning the image in the canonical base ($x_e$) or in the eigenvectors space ($x_v$). Since in the $V$ space the vectors are orthogonal (symetric matrix) it can be proven that the projection on the first 2 vectors of $V$ ($v_1,v_2$) will be just keeping the respective coordinates: $$x_v = a_1 v_1 + ... + a_M$$ $$x_{\text{projected}} = a_1 v_1 + a_2 v_2$$

Some final comments on the movie visualization¶

The principal components spanning the 2 dimensions both when using PC1 and PC2, makes very much intuative sense. We already covered some examples where some movies are mentioned and their correspoding PC value, however it is also interesting to note that movies which are similar form clusters. These are far more accurate in the PC1 and PC2 bokeh plot than they are in the PC4 and PC5 bokeh plot, which comes to us as no surprise. In the bokeh plot above where PC1 and PC2 are the bassis vectors spanning the dimensionality room, we can identify some clusters e.g.:

  • The princess diaries, miss congeniality and just my luck form a cluster: They are the only movies in our visualization in the romcom genre.
  • Edge of tomorrow, the Matrix, Star Wars and Terminator: They are all Futuristic/sci-fi esque movies
  • Shindler's List, American History X, Fight Club, Shawshank Redemption, Cidade de Deus and American Beuty: They are all highly recognized dramas considered to among the best movies in our time.

Seeing these rather clear clusters is a small indication of a sucessfull dimenionalty reduction and that projecting the movies on PC1 and PC2 offer an intuative intepretation. When projecting the movies on PC4 and PC5 the clusters are not at all equally accurate. The distance between some movies whom are diametrically differnet are sometimes very low e.g.:

  • Smultronstället (One of the most regognized movies by swedish director Ingmar Bergman. The movie revolves around very abstract themes like introspection) is pretty much in a cluster with Jurassic park, Edge of Tomorrow , Terminator, etc. We would certainly not put smultronstället in Futuristic/Sci-fi cluster.
  • Shindler's List and the Kings speech almost seem to end up in romcom cluster, a category which they most certainly not are in.

These sort of goofs are not present in the projection of movies on PC1 and PC2 which is not strange at all, them being more important principal components.
